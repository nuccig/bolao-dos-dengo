# ADR-001: Cohesive Mobile-First Demo Journey

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-06-06

## Context

Bolao Dos Dengo needs a visual refresh that follows `DESIGN.md`, feels fluid on mobile, and can be validated with mocked content before live data is available. The product already has the main journey shape: a visitor learns the value proposition, enters or creates a pool, sees matches, submits predictions, and compares ranking position.

Research and product clarification showed that the MVP should balance three outcomes: visual consistency with the BMW M-inspired design direction, a clear mobile journey, and realistic happy-path data for demonstration. The primary success signal is that a person understands the app proposition in under one minute while navigating on a phone.

## Decision

Use a cohesive mobile-first demo journey as the product approach. The MVP will show the complete visitor-to-ranking flow with a realistic active pool scenario: upcoming matches, editable predictions before kickoff, and a live-feeling ranking. The visual language will combine the premium BMW M-inspired system from `DESIGN.md` with subtle football and social competition cues.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Design System First

- **Description**: Prioritize a reusable visual system and component consistency before improving the end-to-end product journey.
- **Pros**: Reduces future inconsistency, creates a stronger foundation for later work, and makes future visual updates easier.
- **Cons**: Delays the user-visible product story and may not prove whether the app is understandable to new users.
- **Why rejected**: The near-term product need is a coherent, demonstrable mobile journey rather than internal design-system completeness.

### Alternative 2: Rich Competition Demo

- **Description**: Create a more detailed demo around competition, achievements, close ranking battles, history, and social signals.
- **Pros**: Makes the product feel more alive and emotionally engaging during demos.
- **Cons**: Expands scope beyond the selected happy path and risks obscuring the core prediction-and-ranking loop.
- **Why rejected**: The MVP should focus on clarity in under one minute, using only the data needed to explain the core value.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Users and stakeholders can understand the product promise quickly on mobile.
- The visual refresh stays anchored to `DESIGN.md` while becoming relevant to World Cup prediction pools.
- Mocked data supports demos and visual validation without requiring production-like data.

### Negative

- Some edge states, achievement depth, and advanced competition storytelling will be deferred.
- A complete design-system extraction may remain incomplete after the MVP.

### Risks

- The BMW M-inspired aesthetic may feel too automotive unless football and social competition cues are applied carefully.
- Happy-path mocked data may hide important empty, locked, or error states if later phases do not address them.

## Implementation Notes

This ADR describes product scope and experience direction only. Technical implementation details belong in the TechSpec.
